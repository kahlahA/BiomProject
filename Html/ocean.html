Ocean

Climate

The ocean biome, covering roughly 70% of the entire Earth's surface, is the largest and most expansive biome by far. It is comprised by its endless sea of salt water and stretches across the globe, ranging in temperatures of near-freezing in the polar regions to warm and vibrant in the tropical regions.

Vegetation

Many different water plants including kelp, seagrass, and algae thrive in the shallower, coastal regions of the ocean, while warm, tropical coasts are home to a vast and diverse ecosystem of coral reefs.

Animal Life

The ocean is home to the most varying and plentiful number of species, with the most notable being whales, sharks, dolphins, squids, and thousands of saltwater fish species.
