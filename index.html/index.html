World Biomes

The Desert

The Desert is an arid region of little precipitation and extreme temperatures ranging from very hot to very cold. Living conditions are harsh with scarce vegetation, yet many animals still make the desert their home.

The Tundra

The Tundra is an extremely frigid and desolate region with little vegetation. Low precipitation in the form of snowfall and frozen landscapes make living in the tundra challenging, but many animals adapt to its harsh environment.

The Forest

The Forest is a lush ecosystem with good precipitation and average temperatures. There are vast amounts of plant life with most notably a dense population of trees, supplying animals with plenty of food and shelter.

The Grassland

The Grassland is a region with vegetation consisting mostly of grasses. Average temperatures and lower precipitation make living conditions not ideal but habitable for the animals that live there.

The Ocean

The Ocean covers almost three quarters of the entire world's surface, making it the most abundant biome by far. It consists of many different plant and animal species that have adapted to the salty environment.

The Rainforest

The Rainforest is a very lush tropical region sprawling with life. Its high temperatures and abundant precipitation allow many various plant and animal species to thrive, and it is the most diversified biome in the world.
